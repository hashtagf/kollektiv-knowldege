# Second Brain Methodology

> A digital, external memory system that captures, organises, distils, and re-uses what you encounter — so your biological brain is freed to do the *thinking*, not the *remembering*.

## Summary

The "Second Brain" framework was popularised by Tiago Forte in *Building a Second Brain* (2022). It treats personal knowledge management as a productivity discipline: not "writing notes," but **maintaining a system that reliably turns raw inputs into reusable creative output**.

Two pillars sit underneath it:

- **[CODE method](./code-method.md)** — the four-stage workflow: **C**apture · **O**rganise · **D**istil · **E**xpress.
- **[PARA method](./para-method.md)** — the storage scheme: **P**rojects · **A**reas · **R**esources · **A**rchive.

Where the [zettelkasten](../concepts/zettelkasten-method.md) is *idea-first* (build a thinking environment), Second Brain is *outcome-first* (build a delivery environment). They're complementary — most teams end up using both layers.

## Key ideas

- **Capture only what resonates.** A second brain is not an inbox; it's a curated set of things you'd actually re-read. If a clipping doesn't make you nod, skip it.
- **Organise by actionability, not topic.** The same article might support a current Project, sit in a long-running Area, or be parked in Resources — its location depends on *what you'll do with it*, not what it's *about*. See [PARA](./para-method.md).
- **Distil progressively.** A note matures through layers — full text → bolds → highlights → executive summary. Future-you should be able to grasp it in 5 seconds. See [progressive summarization](./progressive-summarization.md).
- **Express to learn.** The point of capture is *output*: a doc, a decision, a snippet, a talk. If notes never leave the vault, the system is failing.
- **Just in time, not just in case.** Don't pre-organise for hypothetical futures; let the next concrete project pull old notes into the open.

## Common failure modes

- **Capture without distil.** The vault becomes an unread highlight graveyard. Fix: schedule the [weekly review](./daily-weekly-review.md).
- **Topic-based folders.** Notes get filed where they "belong" but never resurface in a Project. Fix: re-bucket by [PARA](./para-method.md).
- **Perfect-from-the-start syndrome.** Pages are over-edited before they're useful. Fix: rough notes are fine; revisit when the next project pulls them in.
