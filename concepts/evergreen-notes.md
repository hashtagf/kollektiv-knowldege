# Evergreen Notes

> Notes written to be revisited and extended over years, not discarded after one use.

## Summary

Coined by Andy Matuschak, "evergreen" describes a note-quality goal: each note is a tiny, durable, concept-oriented piece of writing that you can safely link from elsewhere, confident it will still be useful years from now. Evergreens are the counterpart to fleeting notes — the ephemeral scribbles, meeting snippets, and raw captures that feed the writing process.

## Key ideas

- **Concept-oriented** — titled by an idea ("evergreen notes") not a source or a date.
- **Atomic** — one concept per note so links stay precise.
- **Densely linked** — evergreens only get better as more notes link in.
- **Written for your future self** — prose, not bullet soup.

## Example

A meeting note that says *"team agreed on TS for the design system"* is fleeting. The decision reasoning — "Design tokens benefit from a typed API because…" — is evergreen, lives in [design-tokens.md](./design-tokens.md), and will be linked from future RFCs.
