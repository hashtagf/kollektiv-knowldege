# Zettelkasten Method

> A note-taking system built around small, atomic, densely linked notes that the writer revisits and extends over time.

## Summary

The zettelkasten ("slip-box" in German) is a PKM approach popularised by sociologist Niklas Luhmann. Instead of long, topic-organised documents, you write many short notes — each with a single idea, a unique identifier, and explicit links to other notes. Over time the connections between notes generate insight that any single note could not hold on its own.

## Key ideas

- **Atomic notes** — one idea per note so it can be linked and reused without dragging in unrelated context.
- **Link-first** — a new note is only valuable when it connects to notes you already have. Links > folders.
- **Emergent structure** — [Maps of Content](../maps/knowledge-management.md) grow from the links, rather than being designed up-front.
- **Your own words** — always write the note as if you were explaining it to a colleague, not quoting a source.

## Example

You read [How to Take Smart Notes](../resources/how-to-take-smart-notes.md) and extract the idea *"writing is thinking."* That becomes its own note, linked to `writing-as-thinking.md`, [evergreen-notes.md](./evergreen-notes.md), and the source. Next week when you're drafting a blog post you follow the link from a note on *deep work* and recover the thought without searching for it.
