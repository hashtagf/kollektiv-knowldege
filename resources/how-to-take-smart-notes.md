# How to Take Smart Notes

> Sönke Ahrens' practical guide to Luhmann's zettelkasten — the book most of this vault's note-taking conventions come from.

## Why it matters

It's the most actionable description in English of the slip-box workflow. If someone new to the vault asks *why we link instead of organising by folder*, point them here first.

## Summary

Key points, in our own words:

- Writing is thinking — you don't *have* ideas and then write them, you write to discover them.
- Keep three kinds of notes: fleeting (capture), literature (source-attached), and permanent (evergreen).
- Each permanent note holds a single idea and earns its place by linking to existing notes.
- Structure emerges from links; it isn't designed up-front.

## Notable quotes

> "You cannot think without writing."
> — Sönke Ahrens
